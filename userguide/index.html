1. Introduction

About Taxease

Taxease is a mobile-first tax management platform designed to help Nigerian employees, freelancers, SMEs, payroll officers, HR teams, accountants, and tax consultants calculate, understand, and manage tax obligations with ease.

The platform simplifies tax calculations by providing transparent PAYE computations, tax breakdowns, payroll processing tools, educational resources, and downloadable reports.

Purpose of This Guide

This guide explains how to use Taxease and its key features.

6. Understanding Tax Results

Gross Income

The total income before any deductions are applied.

Pension Contribution

Employee pension deductions entered during the calculation process.

NHF Contribution

National Housing Fund contribution used during tax computation.

Rent Relief

An eligible relief applied during tax computations where applicable. It reduces taxable income according to applicable tax rules.

Taxable Income

The amount remaining after eligible deductions and relief allowances are applied.

PAYE Tax

The estimated tax payable based on applicable tax bands.

Net Salary

The amount remaining after taxes and deductions have been applied.

11. Tax Glossary

The Glossary provides explanations for common tax terms used throughout the platform.

  • PAYE (Pay As You Earn): The tax deducted directly from your salary by your employer and paid to the government.
  • Taxable Income: The part of your income that actually gets taxed.
  • Relief: A legal deduction that lowers the income you pay tax on.
  • Pension: Retirement savings deducted before tax payment (lowers your tax bill).
  • NHF (National Housing Fund): Housing fund contribution (also lowers your tax).
  • Gross Income: Total earnings before deductions.
  • Taxable Income: The portion of your income left after removing reliefs and contributions. This is what tax is actually calculated on.
  • Net Salary (Take-Home Pay): The amount you actually receive after all deductions.
  • CRA (Consolidated Relief Allowance) : A legal tax reduction. It lowers your taxable income automatically, you don’t pay tax on this portion.
  • Payroll: The process of calculating employee salaries, taxes, pensions, deductions, and payments.
  • Minimum Tax: A small tax amount you pay if your calculated tax is very low or zero to ensure everyone contributes something.
  • Monthly Tax: Your tax calculated per month.
  • Annual Tax: Your tax calculated for a full year.
  • Tax Summary / Report: A downloadable document showing how your tax was calculated. This is helpful for records or loan applications.
  • Tax Bands: The different tax rates for different income ranges.

Recommended Action:
